The Major River Basin boundary defines the land and water relationship for our large river systems. All the watersheds within the same major river basin are hydrologically connected by their streams and lakes. The position of a watershed within its river basin show how it connects to the land and water both upstream and downstream. The condition of the land and water system throughout the basin impacts the health of each watershed.

In-depth system health

This section explores the WHAF's indices of ecological health. Scores for each index are shown on the map and the mean score values for each scale (catchment, upstream, downstream, major watershed and basin) are shown on a gauge, if a location was selected.

This is an opportunity to explore each index in depth, determine whether it is important for your investigation, and at what scale (you can easily zoom to, and change symbology for, relevant scales using the buttons at the bottom). You may also add layers to the map, and keep your own notes about it

Clicking the 'Add to report' button will add this map view, the index description and your notes to the 'Health indices' chapter of your report. You may edit the text and the map view at any time.

If you created a health overview in the previous section and clicked 'View Selected', this section will only show the indices you checked. Otherwise, it goes through all available WHAF indices.
