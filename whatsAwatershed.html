The term ‘watershed’ is used in many ways. A ‘True Watershed’ contains the total land area and water features upstream of a given point on the landscape. By contrast, ‘Major Watersheds’ are administrative units (HUC8) that may artificially divide a larger watershed or major river basin. In Minnesota, more than half of the Major Watersheds are not true watersheds.

In the WHAF Mapping application, DNR Catchments are used as the smallest delineated unit of drainage area. Once a map location is set (using this button ), it is easy to view different spatial scales for that location, including 'Major Watershed', 'True Watershed' (Upstream Area), as well as Downstream Area, River Basin and DNR-catchment.

Watershed Health Assessment Scores were calculated at the level of ‘Major Watershed’ and, where possible, ‘DNR Catchment’. Proper exploration should include using the different scale options to examine the relationship between true watersheds, administrative watersheds and other natural and artificial boundaries.
